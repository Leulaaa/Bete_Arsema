Instrument

krar (Geʽez: ክራር)

The kerar, a five or six-stringed bowl-shaped lyre, originated in Eritrea and Ethiopia. It is a popular folk instrument in these regions, known for its pentatonic scale tuning. The krar is played by plucking, strumming, or bowing the strings, and its tone varies depending on the technique
Read More

Washint (Amharic: ዋሽንት)

The Washint is a traditional Ethiopian end-blown flute, often made of wood or bamboo. It is one of the most common traditional musical instruments in Ethiopia, alongside the Krar (a six-stringed lyre) and the Masenqo (a one-string fiddle). Musicians in Ethiopia have traditionally used the washint to accompany songs and pass down their oral history.
Read More

begena, (Amharic: በገና)

The begena is a 10-stringed box-lyre instrument from Ethiopia, primarily used in the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahdo Church for religious music and meditation. It's often referred to as the "Harp of King David" and is believed by some to be a direct descendant of the harp played by King David. The begena is exclusively used for the zema, a spiritual part of Ethiopian music
